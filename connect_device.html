VACOM POS - Foreground Service Permission Justification

This document provides the justification for the foreground service permissions required by the VACOM POS application. The app is deployed on dedicated Android-based POS devices with integrated, internal Bluetooth printers. Its key functionality is to run a persistent foreground service that listens for print commands over a specified local IP port.

1. FOREGROUND_SERVICE_CONNECTED_DEVICE

Purpose: To maintain a stable, persistent connection with the internal Bluetooth printer.

  • Ensures that the foreground service managing the Bluetooth connection remains active even if the app is moved to the background or killed.
  • Guarantees real-time printing of invoices and receipts by keeping the printer connection reliable and continuous.
  • The printer is an internal device on the POS hardware, and the connection is never exposed to external networks.

2. FOREGROUND_SERVICE_DATA_SYNC

Purpose: To enable continuous data synchronization by keeping an open socket server active.

  • Listens on a pre-defined local IP port (e.g., 15682) to receive printing commands from other devices in the same local area network (LAN).
  • Ensures that the service remains active at all times to process data for print jobs even when the app is not in the foreground.
  • Facilitates secure, real-time data transfer strictly within the local network, with no external sharing of data.
